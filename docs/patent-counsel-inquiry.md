## What I Have Built

The platform delivers:

- Geofence-triggered, route-relative Points of Interest (POI) — both generic and premium sponsored content — to passengers based on their aircraft's live position against the filed flight route
- Real-time turbulence awareness via a novel crowd-sourced reporting mechanism (described below)
- AI conversational interface that adapts content delivery, tone, and depth to a passenger-defined profile

## Novel Elements I Believe Are Patentable

### 1. Route-Relative Geofencing for Airborne Content Delivery

A geofencing system that is route-aware, not merely position-aware. The system distinguishes between a passenger overflying a geographic area versus one landing in it, and delivers contextually appropriate sponsored or generic content accordingly. Content cascades dynamically when in-flight reroutes change the route geometry — new geofences activate, prior geofences deactivate, new content fires without passenger or operator intervention.

### 2. Anxiety-Profile-Weighted Crowd-Sourced Observational Data

A system in which passengers submit real-time cabin-experience turbulence reports (termed FLYREPs) using consumer-language severity anchors. Each report is stamped at submission time with the reporter's self-reported anxiety profile (a 3-tier scale). The anxiety profile is used as a reliability weight in a confidence scoring model that aggregates crowd-sourced reports with official PIREP data and NWS/NOAA model output (GTG). A high-anxiety reporter's severe report is weighted lower than a low-anxiety reporter's equivalent report. The system is designed to evolve the static weight to an empirically derived per-user reliability score in a subsequent version.

### 3. Multi-Source Turbulence Confidence Scoring with Rules-Based Conflict Resolution

A system that aggregates three distinct data sources — crowd-sourced FLYREPs, official pilot PIREPs, and NWS GTG model data — applies time decay consistent with PIREP standards per weather phenomenon type, detects and logs inter-source conflicts, and produces a unified confidence score used to gate and tune passenger notifications. The rules layer is explicitly designed as a human-in-the-loop system rather than a pure ML system, with conflict events logged as a training corpus for future ML calibration.

### 4. AI-Mediated Sponsored Content and Interaction Analytics Platform for Airborne Passengers

A system in which third-party content providers (tourism boards, airlines, travel companies) purchase geofence-triggered AI conversational content delivery to passengers who are physically in geographic proximity to the content provider's area of interest. The system returns interaction analytics — engagement depth, follow-up questions, conversion actions — to content providers as a distinct data product. The combination of captive airborne audience, route-correlated geographic relevance, AI-mediated delivery, and interaction analytics as a returnable data product is believed to be novel as a unified system.
